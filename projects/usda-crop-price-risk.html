US Crop Price &
Supply-Chain Risk

Business question: which US crops are most vulnerable to price volatility and supply disruptions — and can we predict price movement 3–6 months out? A full end-to-end analysis pipeline over 14 years of USDA farm-gate prices and FRED commodity CPI.

  • Corn is the most volatile crop (CV 28.5%), followed by Wheat (25.5%) and Soybeans (20.5%).
  • Colorado, Texas, Kansas and Nebraska are the highest price-risk states.
  • Soybeans are forecast to rise +17% over the next 6 months (Apr–Oct 2026).
  • Farm prices track commodity CPI with r = 0.90–0.96 — making CPI a reliable 4–6 week leading indicator.
