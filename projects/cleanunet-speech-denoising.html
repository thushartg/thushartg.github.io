CleanUNet
Speech Denoising

A CleanUNet-based denoising autoencoder for real-time speech enhancement, trained on the Microsoft DNS Challenge dataset. It operates directly on raw waveforms to preserve phase information — rather than on spectrograms — using gated 1D convolutions and transposed layers.

  • Works on raw time-domain waveforms, preserving phase information that spectrogram methods discard.
  • Encoder–decoder (U-Net style) with gated 1D convolutions and transposed layers for upsampling.
  • Trained and evaluated on the Microsoft DNS Challenge dataset.
  • Achieved significant, audible noise suppression on held-out samples.

Why it matters

Real-time speech enhancement is a core building block for calls, transcription and voice interfaces. Operating on raw waveforms avoids the phase-reconstruction artifacts of spectrogram-based denoisers, producing more natural output — a strong demonstration of applied deep learning on a hard signal-processing problem.
